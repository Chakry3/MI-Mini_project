Abstract:


Technological innovations in the field of medical science have played a vital role in disease prevention and diagnostics. One such advancement is the heart rate monitoring system. Pulse rate is an important health parameter related to the human cardiovascular system. This paper describes a technique for measuring heart rate through a fingertip and displaying the results on both a local console and a global internet site. The system is based on the principle of photoplethysmography (PPG), a noninvasive method for measuring variations in blood volume in tissue using a light source and detector.


Introduction:


Due to the advancement in Technology in the field of Medical Science, it has become much easier to determine different parameters of a patient through electronic machines like heart rate, temperature, etc. One such electronic device is the Heart rate monitoring System. In this project, we will measure different parameters of a patient and display them on the internet. These parameters can be shown on a global as well as a local server. The components required are NODEMCU ESP 8266 and an analog pulse sensor.


Measurement and Calculation of Heart Rate: Heart rate is simply measured by placing the thumb over the pulse sensor for a few seconds until the analog value is received by NODEMCU ESP8266. Heart rate is then taken for 5 seconds to calculate the heart rate per second. These values are then multiplied by 60 to get heart rate in bpm (beats per minute). This method, although simple, is not always accurate and can give errors, especially when the heart rate is high.


For a healthy adult, the ordinary heart rate is in the range of 60 to 100 bpm (beats per minute).

Athlete’s heartbeats generally range from 40 to 60 bpm depending on their fitness level. If a person's heart rate is constantly over 100 beats per minute, then the person is said to have a higher heart rate, which is also known as tachyarrhythmia. It can reduce the efficiency of the heart by reducing the amount of blood pumped through the body and can result in chest pain and lightheadedness. With the advancement in technology, it is easy to monitor the patient's heart rate even at home. IoT is a technology of network mechanisms to collect and share information from the world ubiquitously, and it can be managed for various purposes.


Internet of Things (IoT): IoT is a concept where data collection is done via devices that communicate with each other and store that data in the cloud. This data can be extracted, analyzed, and transmitted much faster and more efficiently. IoT has paved the way for new possibilities in various areas and industries.

Implementation:


  1. Wifi Module (NodeMcu): NodeMcu is a very good Wi-Fi module for IoT applications. It includes firmware that runs on the ESP8266 Wi-Fi SoC from Espressif Systems, and hardware based on the ESP-12 module. NodeMCU development board is an open-source board based on the ESP8266EX microcontroller with an integrated Wi-Fi transceiver. Programming and uploading in NodeMCU from any computer via a microUSB port are straightforward as it supports several programming languages, making NodeMCU a smart choice for IoT.


  2. Pulse Sensor: For NODEMCU, the pulse sensor is a plug-and-play heart rate sensor. It can be utilized by anyone who wants to incorporate real-time heart rate information into their projects. The sensor detects the movement of blood through the finger and provides a numerical output of the heart rate once a finger is positioned on it.



  3. NODEMCU ESP8266 and Pulse Sensor Interfacing:


    1. Connect a 5V power supply to NODEMCU ESP8266.

    2. Connect a USB cable from the computer to NODEMCU ESP8266 to download the program.

    3. Connect the VCC (+) pin of the pulse sensor to the 3V3 pin of NODEMCU.

    4. Connect the Signal pin of the pulse sensor to the A0 pin of NODEMCU.

    5. Connect the GND (-) pin of the pulse sensor to the 3V3 pin of NODEMCU.

Results & Discussion:


The Pulse Sensor is attached to a body organ, such as a finger or ear, where it can easily detect the pulse. It measures changes in blood volume that occur with each heartbeat, causing variations in light intensity through the organ. The NODEMCU then converts these changes into heartbeats per minute (BPM). The ESP8266 accepts pulse data from the sensor and sends the data to a webpage or the internet. It connects to the network using the router provided in the code and transmits the sensor data online.


Advantages:


  • The circuit is simple and cost-effective.

  • The entire system is small and portable, making it easy to assemble and reassemble as needed.


    Disadvantages:


  • Sensor calibration is required.

  • The pulse sensor is dedicated for specific uses.

  • The lifespan of the pulse sensor is relatively short.

Conclusion:


In our IoT-based heart rate monitoring system, patient heart rate data is collected using a fingertip sensor through a NODEMCU board. This data is transmitted to both a global and a local server. The local server is created using an HTML page, while the global server is accessible through a webpage.
